My Favourite Projects

Just like those in the classrooms around me, I have done many projects over the years. My favourites of which are:

1. The Standard model of Particles, 2. Oceans, and 3. The Zoo

Theses are my most memorable projects to date and were by far the most fun (for different reasons).

The Standard Model of Particles

This is by far my favourite project I have worked on ever. And there are a lot of reasons as to why. The first being that I went into this topic completly blind. This was my assigned ISU for grade 12 physics. My teacher wanted us to have to self teach a topic on something we likely had little to no real understanding of. In my case, I had never even heard of the Standard Model. This meant that I would have to do a lot of research. And normally this wasn't something I was fond of. But I had managed to come across some great resources to help myself understand what the Stadard Model is. These publishes made it 'easy' to understand. Something that also made this a memorable project was the fact that by the end of I had had my eyes opened to what everything really is. I put a lot more 'effort' into this project and got a lot more out of it than I had ever expected to. This is what makes it my favourite project.

Oceans

My next favourite project was my grade 9 science ISU. This project has a few similar reasons to the Standard Model project. Firstly, this project incomparison is waaaaay more relaxed. I mean it is grade 9 vs. grade 12 so it makes sense that this would be easier. Secondly, just like with the Standard Model, i learnt a lot from this project. Now, what I learned was nowhere near as in depth or as specific but I learned nonehteless. I got to find a lot of facts and present on many different aspects. I mean, afterall my topic was literally 'Oceans'. A little broad if you ask me but I managed.
